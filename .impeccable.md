## Design Context

### Users
NBA bettors, heavy Reddit overlap. Skeptical, sharp, pattern-recognition people who smell inauthenticity instantly. Primarily mobile — checking picks before tip-off, scrolling r/sportsbook between games. They've seen every generic betting site and will bounce if LOCKIN looks like one. They respect conviction and receipts over flashy promises.

### Brand Personality
**Confident. Exclusive. Sharp.**

Voice is direct, never salesy. Speaks like someone who's done the work and knows what they found — not trying to convince you, just showing the edge. No hype words, no "guaranteed" language, no exclamation marks. The confidence is quiet and earned.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark-mode-only, court-black canvas with money-green as the singular accent of conviction. Gold reserved for secondary emphasis (track record, social proof). The visual tone is premium sportsbook intelligence — closer to a Bloomberg terminal for NBA than a DraftKings casino floor.

**Current palette (locked in):**
- Court Black `#0a0e1a` / `#070b14`
- Money Green `#00c853`
- Gold `#ffd700`
- Alert Red `#ff3b3b`
- Silver Gray `#8b92a5`
- Pure White `#f5f5f3`

**Fonts (locked in):** Space Grotesk (display), Inter (body), JetBrains Mono (data)

**Anti-references:** AI slop — no cyan-on-dark, no purple gradients, no glassmorphism, no gradient text, no generic card grids with drop shadows, no "powered by AI" aesthetic. If a Reddit user would screenshot it and post "this looks AI-generated," it's wrong.

### Design Principles
1. **Conviction over decoration** — Every visual element should communicate certainty or information. No ornamental filler.
2. **Earned trust** — Show track record, show the work. Social proof and transparency beat polish.
3. **Reddit-proof** — If it looks like every other AI betting site, it fails. Authenticity and craft over trendy effects.
4. **Mobile-first intensity** — The primary experience is a phone screen before tip-off. Fast, scannable, zero friction.
5. **Less is locked** — Exclusivity through restraint. The blurred pick, the limited questions, the gated content — scarcity is the design language of value.
